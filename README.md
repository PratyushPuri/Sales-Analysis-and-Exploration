# Comprehensive E-Commerce Sales Analysis

## Overview
I am absolutely thrilled to present this end-to-end E-Commerce Data Analysis project, which tackles a highly complex and fragmented dataset distributed across multiple relational CSV files. This dual-technology project seamlessly integrates SQL for robust database querying and Python for advanced Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and visualization. By bridging the gap between raw, unstructured data and visual business intelligence, this project uncovers deep, actionable insights into customer purchasing behaviors, product performance metrics, and overall revenue trajectories over time. The repository contains all the code, queries, and presentation materials required to understand the complete data lifecycle.

## Problem Statement
The primary challenge of this project was to extract meaningful business intelligence from a vast, decentralized e-commerce dataset containing distinct information on customers, sellers, products, orders, order items, payments, and geolocation mappings. The objective was meticulously structured into a tiered set of business questions, ranging from basic operational metrics to highly advanced strategic calculations. The ultimate goal was to not only compute these critical metrics accurately using relational database techniques but also to visualize the underlying patterns and correlations. This dual approach ensures that the findings facilitate highly informed, data-driven decision-making for executive and operational stakeholders.

## SQL Analysis 

> Resultant File is `Sales Analysis.sql`

A highly structured and rigorous approach was utilized to query the database, employing everything from basic aggregations to complex Common Table Expressions (CTEs) and Window Functions. The SQL phase successfully addressed multiple tiers of business intelligence:

* **Basic Operations:** Executed foundational queries to extract unique customer locations, calculate total order volumes across specific years, identify the complete range of product categories, and track orders paid via multiple installments to understand baseline operational scale and consumer financing preferences.
* **Intermediate Operations:** Conducted time-series analysis to evaluate monthly order trends and calculate the average number of products per order. Additionally, computed the total revenue generated by each distinct product category and identified the top five customers based on lifetime total spending.
* **Advanced Operations:** * Engineered a sophisticated query using CTEs to calculate the customer retention rate, specifically isolating and identifying users who successfully made repeat purchases within a six-month window of their initial order.
  * Utilized window functions, specifically `ROW_NUMBER()`, to accurately rank and extract the top three highest-spending customers for each respective year, allowing for the tracking of high-value client retention.

## Python Exploratory Data Analysis

> Resultant File is `Sales_EDA.ipynb`

Following the rigorous SQL data extraction and manipulation phase, a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis was conducted using Python's leading data science libraries (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn) within a Jupyter Notebook environment. Key analytical steps included:

* **Data Integration & Cleaning:** Systematically loaded and merged multiple raw datasets (including orders, items, payments, and customers) to create a unified, analytical dataframe. Handled missing values meticulously and ensured all data types were optimized for accurate time-series analysis.
* **Trend Visualization:** Plotted extensive seasonal and monthly order trends to visually communicate peak purchasing periods, seasonal slumps, and overall year-over-year growth trajectories.
* **Geographical Mapping:** Visualized the spatial distribution of the customer base across various states and cities, prominently highlighting the strongest geographical markets and identifying potential areas for expansion.
* **Statistical Visualizations:** Tracked and plotted the moving average of order values over time to smooth out daily volatility and clearly reveal long-term consumer purchasing habits. 
* **Correlation Analysis:** Deeply explored the statistical relationship between product price points and purchase frequencies to understand consumer price sensitivity and market demand elasticity.

## Recommendations
Based on the foundational discoveries and metric calculations performed during the comprehensive analysis, the following strategic actions are highly recommended to drive growth:

* **Leverage Predictive Analytics:** The cleaned data and transformed variables gathered during this EDA are now perfectly primed for robust feature engineering. It is strongly recommended to build and deploy predictive machine learning models to accurately forecast future sales volumes and optimize supply chain and inventory management.
* **Optimize Pricing Strategies:** The detailed correlation insights between product price and purchase frequency should be immediately utilized to test and implement dynamic pricing models. This will maximize sales volume on high-elasticity products while fiercely protecting profit margins on premium, low-elasticity items.
* **Targeted Retention Campaigns:** With the six-month retention rate strictly established via advanced SQL modeling, marketing teams should deploy highly targeted, automated re-engagement campaigns around the five-month mark for new users. This will actively combat customer churn and significantly boost the lifetime value (LTV) of the customer base.
* **Geographic Expansion & Logistics:** Marketing spend should be aggressively reallocated to capitalize on the strongest geographical markets identified in the spatial distribution analysis. Simultaneously, operations teams should investigate and resolve logistical bottlenecks in underperforming regions to improve delivery times and customer satisfaction.
